\subsection*{Solution}
If Carlos took $70\%$ of the pie, there must be $(100 - 70)\% = 30\%$ left. After Maria takes $\frac{1}{3}$ of the remaining $30\%, \ 1 - \frac{1}{3} = \frac{2}{3}$ of the remaining $30\%$ is left. Therefore, the answer is $30\% \cdot \frac{2}{3} = \boxed{\textbf{(C)}\ 20\%}.$ ~Awesome2.1 (Solution) ~quacker88 ( $\LaTeX$ Adjustments)
