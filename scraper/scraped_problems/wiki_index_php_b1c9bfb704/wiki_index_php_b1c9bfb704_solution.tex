\subsection*{Solution}
The ratio between the radius and the arc length is constant. For the inner circle, the radius, which we will denote as $r$ , has a corresponding arc length of $2\pi r - 2\pi$ (the circumference minus the major arc length). For the outer circle, the radius, which is $r + 2\pi$ , has a corresponding arc length of $2\pi$ . We therefore write the equation \[\frac{r}{2\pi r - 2\pi} = \frac{r+2\pi}{2\pi},\] which simplifies to \[r = (r+2\pi)(r-1)\] \[0 = r^2 + (2\pi-2)r - 2\pi.\] Applying the Quadratic Formula, we get that $r =\boxed{\textbf{(B)} 1 - \pi + \sqrt{1 +\pi^2}}$ ~lprado
