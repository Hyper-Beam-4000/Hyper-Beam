\subsection*{Problem}
A rectangular box $\mathcal{P}$ has distinct edge lengths $a$ , $b$ , and $c$ . The sum of the lengths of all $12$ edges of $\mathcal{P}$ is $13$ , the areas of all $6$ faces of $\mathcal{P}$ is $\frac{11}{2}$ , and the volume of $\mathcal{P}$ is $\frac{1}{2}$ . What is the length of the longest interior diagonal connecting two vertices of $\mathcal{P}$ ? $\textbf{(A)}~2\qquad\textbf{(B)}~\frac{3}{8}\qquad\textbf{(C)}~\frac{9}{8}\qquad\textbf{(D)}~\frac{9}{4}\qquad\textbf{(E)}~\frac{3}{2}$
