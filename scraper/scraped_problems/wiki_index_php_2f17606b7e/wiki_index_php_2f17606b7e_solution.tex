\subsection*{Solution}
From the fourth equation we get $d=\frac{30}{abc}.$ Substitute this into the third equation and you get $abc + \frac{30(ab + bc + ca)}{abc} = abc - \frac{120}{abc} = 14$ . Hence $(abc)^2 - 14(abc)-120 = 0$ . Solving, we get $abc = -6$ or $abc = 20$ . From the first and second equation, we get $ab + bc + ca = ab-3c = -4 \Longrightarrow ab = 3c-4$ . If $abc=-6$ , substituting we get $c(3c-4)=-6$ . If you try solving this you see that this does not have real solutions in $c$ , so $abc$ must be $20$ . So $d=\frac{3}{2}$ . Since $c(3c-4)=20$ , $c=-2$ or $c=\frac{10}{3}$ . If $c=\frac{10}{3}$ , then the system $a+b=-3$ and $ab = 6$ does not give you real solutions. So $c=-2$ . Since you already know $d=\frac{3}{2}$ and $c=-2$ , so you can solve for $a$ and $b$ pretty easily and see that $a^{2}+b^{2}+c^{2}+d^{2}=\frac{141}{4}$ . So the answer is $\boxed{145}$ . ~math31415926535 ~minor edit by Mathkiddie
