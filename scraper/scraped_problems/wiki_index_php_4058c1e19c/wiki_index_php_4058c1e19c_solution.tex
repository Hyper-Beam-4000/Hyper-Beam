\section*{{2019 AMC 12A Problems/Problem 19}}
Source: AoPS Wiki

\subsection*{Solution}
Notice that by the Law of Sines, $a:b:c = \sin{A}:\sin{B}:\sin{C}$ , so let's flip all the cosines using $\sin^{2}{x} + \cos^{2}{x} = 1$ ( $\sin{x}$ is positive for $0^{\circ} < x < 180^{\circ}$ , so we're good there). $\sin A=\frac{3\sqrt{15}}{16}, \qquad \sin B= \frac{\sqrt{15}}{8}, \qquad \text{and} \qquad\sin C=\frac{\sqrt{15}}{4}$ These are in the ratio $3:2:4$ , so our minimal triangle has side lengths $2$ , $3$ , and $4$ . $\boxed{\textbf{(A) } 9}$ is our answer.
